# Sprint 2 — Digital Foundations & Number Systems

> **Bolton chapters:** 3–4
> **Time budget:** 5–7 hours
> **Core concept:** PLCs think in bits — fluency in binary, octal, hex, and BCD is the price of admission to addressing memory, decoding I/O, and reading manufacturer documentation.

---

## 🎯 Sprint Goals

- Convert fluently between denary (decimal), binary, octal, hex, and BCD
- Understand 1's-complement and 2's-complement representations of negative numbers
- Recognize bit, nibble, byte, word, and double-word in PLC data structures
- Read and predict the bit pattern of any I/O address on Siemens, Allen-Bradley, and Mitsubishi
- Explain when continuous I/O update is used vs. mass I/O copying
